Who is Geralt?

Overview

Geralt, is a Witcher from the School of the Wolf. Although it is unknown how he ended up at the location of the school in Kaer Morhen as a young child, Geralt was subjected to the mutations forced upon children which created the breed known as witchers. Many children do not survive this change to their body and often die painful and long-lasting deaths. But Geralt, showed extraordinary resistances to the mutations and was immediately but under additional mutations that were very rare in the Witcher breed. Due to these mutations, Geralt was stronger, faster and had all around better reflexes than any other witcher before. But the consequences were that Geralt retained his emotions while everyone else was stripped of them and his hair turned Ash White. Hence his nickname 'The White Wolf'.

Geralt's most notable Feats

He is known consistently as the best swordsman in the North. His skills with a blade are unmatched, and his ability to use the Witcher Signs make him the most lethal combination that the world has seen.

While Witcher's often have the stereotype of being mindless killers who's only sharpness comes from their sword, Geralt is known as a tactical master and can create a battle plan that rivals the best Kings in the land. He was even capable of outsmarting a demon known as Gaunter O'Dimm, Master of Riddles. It is unknown if Gaunter is a standard demon, or the Devil himself.

Geralt's most notable feat is the empathy he has for the monsters' he hunts. While most witchers will killed monsters without a thought and collect their reward and move on due to the mutations removing their emotions, Geralt uses the emotions he maintained to help save the monster if possible. He understands that most monsters are mindless creatures and are often seen as evil creatures because they are different from humans. Geralt will judge the monster by its actions, before making a decision on how to handle the monster. This will often result in Geralt helping the monster find a safe place to live, or break them of whatever curse they've been put under so that it can resume its life in peace. Because, Geralt knows that monsters are thoughtful creatures as well who mostly want to ensure their own safety and are often persecuted by the true monsters of the world, Humans. Geralt does not believe that every issue should be solved by the edge of a blade.
